# Axon First Alignment

## What Is Axon First?

Axon First is Axon's internal build-before-buy initiative — the formal successor to two prior programs:

- **DOVE (Department of Vendor Efficiency)** — brought discipline to vendor engagement
- **DOLE (Department of License Efficiency)** — audited license spend and eliminated redundancy

Both DOVE and DOLE revealed a deeper problem: vendors have made Axon's inbox their digital billboard, and every unsolicited email represents real time and energy cost.

Axon First establishes a three-question framework that must be answered before any vendor engagement:

1. **Can we build it?**
2. **Can we prototype it?**
3. **Build or leverage?**

Claude Enterprise is positioned as the accelerant — enabling rapid prototyping in days rather than vendor evaluation cycles. The first success story was Endpoint Experience Management: capturing Zoom client performance metrics with a live dashboard, avoiding vendor evaluation, procurement, and license costs entirely.

## Portal Alignment with Axon First

The federation portal is a textbook Axon First engagement. The three questions apply directly:

1. **Can Axon build it?** Parts of it, yes — the basic portal is buildable internally. But the hard parts are domain-specific: the OIN Index, SP infrastructure, and the operational experience of federated identity at scale. These represent years of specialized investment that would be inefficient to replicate.

2. **Can Axon prototype it?** Yes — and the co-development model (Option 3) means the prototype becomes the product. There is no throwaway prototype followed by a vendor procurement cycle. The work done in prototyping carries forward directly.

3. **Build or leverage?** The co-development model captures both. Axon gets source code (build ethos). Gesherna brings domain expertise (leverage). Axon earns revenue share (investment, not expense).

## How Co-Development Reinforces Axon First

Option 3 transforms IT spend into investment. Every external engagement becomes a potential revenue stream. This is the logical extension of Axon First: not just "build before you buy" but "build AND earn."

The co-development model means Axon retains the source code and the capability to operate independently, satisfying the build imperative. Gesherna's domain expertise accelerates delivery past what an internal build would achieve on its own timeline, satisfying the leverage imperative. And the revenue share component means the engagement generates returns rather than just consuming budget.

Option 3 establishes a reusable framework for how Axon engages external vendors in the AI era. Instead of paying full price for an IP asset with diminishing defensibility, Axon invests at a discount and earns ongoing revenue from commercialization. Every external engagement becomes a potential revenue stream, not just a cost center. This fundamentally changes Axon's posture toward external development — from "minimize vendor spend" to "find more problems worth solving, because each one is an investment." The co-development model doesn't slow Axon's AI-first initiative. It accelerates it.

## Gesherna's Complementary Role

Gesherna brings specialized domain expertise — federated identity, OIN data, SP infrastructure — that complements Axon's internal engineering. The co-development model makes Gesherna a partner in the Axon First framework, not a traditional vendor.

As the Axon First document itself frames it: *"As your potential customer, I am also your potentially largest competitor. Vendors are ready to compete with each other. They are not ready for this."* Gesherna's model is designed for exactly this customer. The co-development structure means Gesherna succeeds when Axon builds, not when Axon buys.

Gesherna does not compete with Axon's internal build capability. The domain expertise Gesherna brings — the OIN Index as a curated data product, the SP infrastructure for programmatic federation, and the operational experience of federated identity at scale — is complementary to what Axon's engineering teams would build internally. The co-development model ensures both parties are aligned: Axon builds and retains capability, Gesherna accelerates delivery and brings specialized knowledge, and both earn from the result.
