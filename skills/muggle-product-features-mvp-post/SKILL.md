# Post-MVP Intelligence Layer — Features

The Post-MVP Intelligence Layer comprises two features that build on top of the Phase 1 MVP (Config Exchange and App Integration Creation). Neither replaces the MVP — they enhance it. Manual entry remains the fallback at all times. The core problem (removing the infra team from the critical path of app federation) is solved by the MVP without AI and without vendor-facing infrastructure. These features make the process smarter and smoother once the foundation is proven and the user flows are validated.

Both features are planned for the Post-MVP Intelligence Layer. Both depend on Phase 1 MVP. Both are further enhanced when Phase 2's OIN Index becomes available.

---

## Vendor Collaboration via Magic Links

### What It Is and How It Works

Vendor collaboration introduces magic links — tokenized, time-limited URLs that grant a vendor contact access to fill in their side of a federation configuration without needing an Okta account or Axon credentials.

The flow:

1. The Axon employee who owns the integration generates a magic link from within the portal. The link is scoped to a specific integration and expires after a configurable time window.
2. The employee sends the magic link to their vendor contact.
3. The vendor clicks the link and lands on a **vendor-facing portal page** — a scoped interface where they see only the fields relevant to their side of the federation configuration. They have no access to Okta, the Axon-facing portal, or any other integration.
4. The vendor enters the service provider details directly.
5. When the vendor completes their side, the Axon employee is **notified** and the configuration is updated in the portal automatically.

If the link expires before the vendor completes their side, the employee can generate a new one.

### Key Details and Mechanics

**Token management:** Magic links are time-limited and scoped to a specific integration. Three token operations are supported:
- **Expiry** — links expire after a configurable time window.
- **Regeneration** — expired links can be regenerated by the Axon employee.
- **Revocation** — active links can be revoked.

**Stateful, iterative configuration tracking:** Federation is rarely a one-shot process. The portal supports back-and-forth between the Axon employee and their vendor contact. The portal tracks the state of each integration as a conversation, not a one-time form submission. Each update from either side is recorded. Changes are diffed. The portal maintains a clear picture of what has been provided, what has changed, and what is still outstanding.

This differs from the MVP config exchange, which is a structured, one-directional flow: the user enters SP details, the portal returns IdP details. The iterative exchange introduces stateful, conversation-style tracking — each update from either side is recorded, changes are diffed, and the portal maintains a chronological record of every exchange (what was provided, what was updated, and by whom). Both the Axon employee and the vendor (via their magic link session) can see the current state and the full history of changes.

**Why post-MVP:** Magic links require a vendor-facing interface, token management infrastructure, and notification plumbing that are outside the scope of the core MVP. A lightweight version of configuration exchange exists in MVP, but the more intelligent, conversational tracking is post-MVP.

### Deliverables

- **Magic link generation** — Axon employees generate tokenized, time-limited URLs scoped to a specific integration. Vendors use the link to access a vendor-facing portal and provide SP details directly.
- **Vendor-facing portal** — A scoped interface accessible via magic link. Vendors see only the fields relevant to their side of the federation configuration. No Okta account or Axon credentials required.
- **Stateful, iterative configuration tracking** — The portal tracks the state of each integration as a conversation, not a one-time form submission. Each update from either side is recorded with a chronological history of what was provided, what changed, and by whom.
- **Notification on vendor completion** — The Axon employee is notified when the vendor completes their side of the configuration exchange.
- **Token management** — Magic links are time-limited and scoped. Expired links can be regenerated. Revocation is supported.

### Success Criteria

- Vendors can complete their side of a federation configuration without an Okta account or Axon credentials.
- The Axon employee can track the full history of configuration exchanges for a given integration.
- Time-to-federation is reduced by eliminating manual back-and-forth over email or tickets.

### Interaction with Claude Intelligence

When combined with the Claude Intelligence feature, the iterative exchange becomes AI-mediated. Claude interprets vendor documentation, maps vendor-specific terminology to Okta fields, and flags mismatches between what the vendor provided and what Okta expects. Instead of the Axon employee manually translating between the vendor's language and Okta's field names, Claude handles the translation and surfaces only the items that need human attention. As the vendor provides or updates SP details (via magic link or otherwise), Claude reviews the submission, flags issues, and suggests corrections — reducing the number of round trips required to reach a working configuration.

### Interaction with Phase 2's OIN Index

When Phase 2's OIN Index is available, vendor-provided details submitted through the collaboration portal can be validated against known-good configurations. If a vendor's SP details diverge from the OIN template for that application, the portal flags the discrepancy and asks targeted follow-up questions rather than silently accepting a potentially incorrect configuration.

**Phase 2 deliverables specific to vendor collaboration:**

- **OIN-backed validation of vendor submissions** — Vendor-provided SP details are compared against the OIN template for that application. Discrepancies are flagged and surfaced to both the vendor and the Axon employee.
- **Targeted follow-up prompts** — When vendor-provided details diverge from the OIN template, the portal generates specific follow-up questions rather than generic error messages.

**Phase 2 success criteria:**

- Misconfigured vendor submissions are caught before they reach the Okta integration, reducing failed federation attempts.
- Vendors receive clear, actionable guidance when their details do not match expected values.

## Claude-Powered Documentation Parsing and Intelligent Mediation

### What It Is and How It Works

Claude Intelligence has two complementary capabilities: **documentation-first onboarding** and **intelligent mediation**.

**Documentation-first onboarding:** The user provides a link to (or upload of) vendor setup documentation. Claude reads the documentation, determines the protocol (SAML or OIDC), extracts the SP details, and presents a pre-filled, protocol-appropriate form for the user to confirm. Manual entry becomes the fallback rather than the default. The user never needs to understand the difference between SAML and OIDC or hunt for an ACS URL in a PDF.

**Intelligent mediation:** Claude mediates the iterative configuration exchange between the Axon employee and the vendor. Rather than the employee manually translating between vendor-specific terminology and Okta's expected fields, Claude interprets vendor setup documentation, maps vendor-specific field names to Okta's field names, flags mismatches, and guides both sides toward a working configuration.

### Key Details and Mechanics

**Documentation parsing:** Claude can process vendor setup guides, knowledge base articles, and PDF documentation. The user provides a URL or uploads the document directly. Claude parses the content, identifies the federation protocol, and extracts the relevant configuration values:
- **SAML:** Entity ID, ACS URL, certificate details
- **OIDC:** redirect URIs, client configuration

**Pre-filled forms:** Claude pre-fills what it can and clearly indicates which fields it was unable to extract. The form always shows the user exactly what Claude found and what is still needed. The user can fill in remaining fields manually or go back to the vendor for missing details.

**Manual entry as fallback:** The intelligence layer enhances Phase 1 — it does not replace it. Manual entry remains available as a fallback at all times. Claude pre-fills and validates; the user confirms. If the user prefers to enter details manually, or if Claude cannot parse the vendor's documentation, the full manual flow from the MVP is always available.

**Specific mismatch types Claude catches:**
- **Wrong certificate format** — e.g., DER vs. PEM, missing headers
- **Trailing slashes or protocol mismatches in Entity IDs and URLs** — trailing slashes in Entity IDs, protocol mismatches in URLs
- **Attribute name discrepancies** — e.g., vendor expects "emailAddress" but Okta sends "email"
- **Protocol mismatches** — vendor documentation describes SAML but the user selected OIDC, or vice versa
- **Missing or misformatted redirect URIs** for OIDC configurations

**Why post-MVP:** The MVP delivers value immediately with a clean, guided manual flow. Intelligence is layered on top once the core portal is proven, the user flows are validated, and the foundation is stable. This ensures the product delivers value from day one and that the intelligence layer enhances a working product rather than being a prerequisite for one.

### Deliverables

- **Documentation parsing and protocol detection** — Claude reads vendor setup documentation (URL or uploaded file), determines whether the federation protocol is SAML or OIDC, and selects the appropriate configuration form.
- **SP detail extraction and form pre-fill** — Claude extracts service provider details (ACS URL, Entity ID, redirect URIs, certificates, etc.) from vendor documentation and presents a pre-filled form for the user to confirm. Fields that could not be extracted are clearly marked for manual entry.
- **Intelligent mediation of vendor exchanges** — Claude participates in the iterative configuration exchange, interpreting vendor-specific terminology, mapping it to Okta's expected fields, and surfacing discrepancies for human resolution.
- **Mismatch detection and guided resolution** — Claude flags common configuration errors (wrong certificate format, trailing slashes in Entity IDs, attribute name discrepancies, protocol mismatches) and provides specific guidance on how to resolve them.

### Success Criteria

- Users can complete a federation setup by providing a link to vendor documentation rather than manually entering SP details.
- The number of failed federation attempts due to misconfiguration is measurably reduced.
- Round trips between the Axon employee and vendor during configuration exchange are reduced.

### Interaction with Vendor Collaboration

When the Vendor Collaboration feature is active, Claude participates in the iterative exchange. As the vendor provides or updates SP details (via magic link or otherwise), Claude reviews the submission, flags issues, and suggests corrections — reducing the number of round trips required to reach a working configuration. The iterative exchange becomes AI-mediated: Claude interprets vendor documentation, maps vendor-specific terminology to Okta fields, and flags mismatches between what the vendor provided and what Okta expects. The Axon employee no longer needs to manually translate between the vendor's language and Okta's field names — Claude handles the translation and surfaces only the items that need human attention.

### Interaction with Phase 2's OIN Index

When Phase 2's OIN Index is available, Claude's intelligence becomes significantly more targeted. Claude can validate vendor-provided details against known-good configurations for that application. If the vendor's SP details diverge from the OIN template, Claude identifies the specific discrepancy and asks targeted follow-up questions — for example: *"The OIN template for this app expects the ACS URL at /sso/saml, but the vendor provided /saml/acs. Which is correct for your instance?"*

**Phase 2 deliverables specific to Claude Intelligence:**

- **OIN-backed validation** — Claude compares vendor-provided details against the OIN template for that application. Discrepancies between the vendor's submission and the known-good configuration are flagged with specific context.
- **Targeted follow-up questions** — When vendor-provided details diverge from the OIN template, Claude generates precise follow-up questions (e.g., "The OIN template expects the ACS URL at /sso/saml, but the vendor provided /saml/acs. Which is correct for your instance?") rather than generic validation errors.

**Phase 2 success criteria:**

- Claude can distinguish between a vendor submission that is wrong and one that is intentionally different from the OIN template.
- Follow-up questions are specific enough that the vendor or employee can resolve them in a single response.
